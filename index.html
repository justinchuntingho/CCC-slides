Meanwhile in Hong Kong...


I aim to...

Understand Hong Kong nationalism against the context of a world of nations

A World of Nations

  • Nations are interconnected, interdependent, and interacting with each other
  • A key task for stateless nations is to project the nation into international arenas
  • Hong Kong interacts not only with China but other nations
  • Previous works placed too much focus on the relationship between Hong Kong and China

Data

Data

  • Nationalist Dataset:
    Facebook page of key nationalist organisations in Hong Kong (8,783 posts)
  • Comparison Dataset:
    Facebook posts of all major political parties in Hong Kong (16,823 posts)

Why Facebook?

  • An important platform of political communication (Michael Chan, Hsuan-Ting Chen & Francis L.F. Lee, 2016;
    Hsuan-Ting Chen, Michael Chan & Francis L.F. Lee, 2016;
    Francis L.F. Lee & Joseph Man Chan, 2015; Gary Tang & Francis L.F. Lee, 2013)
  • A major channel of mobilization during the Umbrella Movement (Paul S. N. Lee, Clement Y. K. So & Louis Leung, 2015)
  • All nationalist groups use Facebook

Comparing Political Camps

  • Hong Kong as "global city"
  • High affiliation to the identity of "global citizen"
  • International elements in the Hong Kong identity
  • International outreach of past pro-democracy movements
  • A baseline for internationalism while holding political culture constant

Internationalism

  • Inward-internationalism: the willingness to accept foreign influence
  • Outward-internationalism: the attempts to project the nation in international arenas and invite international cooperation

Inward-internationalism

Inward-internationalism

  • Measured by counting the mentions of foreign countries
  • Expect the nationalists to mention foreign countries less frequently than other political camps



International Experience


Colonialism

  • Early development of Hong Kong's social institution and autonomy are attributed to British colonialism
  • "Colonial legacies" are acknowledged and celebrated

Outward-internationalism

Outward-internationalism

  • Measured by English posts
  • As an indicator of the intention to communicate with an international audience
  • Expect the nationalists to publish less posts in English than other political camps



Appeals to international actors

  • Foreign Consulate Generals and government officials
  • US-HK Policy Act, Causeway Bay Books, and other political events

Oversea Visits

  • Outreach to to various international actors
  • Seeking aid and inviting foreign intervention
  • Hong Kong as an aspiring member of the free world and a potential ally for other stateless-nations

Discussion

  • Different manifestations of nationalism between the relationship with China and other others
  • Stateless nations in authoritarian context might have different goals for international outreach
  • Manifestation of nationalism is contingent on political context
